TREKKING IN INDIA

Trekking take you to very close to the natural landscape. Trekking also gives you unique chance to explore the culture and the people of mountain range in India. Trekking in India is very famous throughout the world. The trekkers from remotest corner of the world come to take life time experience of Indian Trekking Treks. North Indian trekking destination is somewhat similar to paradise for trekkers. The famous trekking destinations of India are Ladakh, Himachal, Sikkim and Garhwal. All these are in Himalayan belt. Himalayas are taken as the best trekking destination in all over the world. The Himalayan foothills are just ideal for trekking, Hiking and Camping offering energetic out-door people a different and exciting option for a adventure holiday.

WHY INDIA IS A GREAT TREKKING DESTINATION?

India is considered is a hub for mountain trekking as it offers plenty of trekking opportunities in its diverse geographical regions. The vast Himalayan ranges in long stretch while its huge landscape is ideal for best trekking tours in India. Every diverse regions of India guarantee unique experience. If trekking in the Himalayas of Uttarakhand offers you picturesque views of valleys, Sikkim Himalayas promises panoramic views of Mount Kanchenjunga while a trek to Ladakh Himalayas brings you face to face with bizarre beauty of this region. Visiting thousands years old monasteries enlightens you while roaming on the Zanskar River is thrilling. Planning a trek across Garhwal Himalayas is amalgamation of spirituality and adventure. In some part of the India is ready to teach you traditional culture and lifestyle of people.

Latest Trek News


40% bookings cancelled for trekking in Uttarkashi.


UTTARKASHI: The recent avalanche tragedy at Mount Draupadi ka Danda-ll -- that left 27 young mountaineers dead and two others missing -- has dealt a major blow to the adventure sports sector in Uttarkashi district.
A hub for trekking and mountaineering activities, the hill district where the tragedy unfolded, is witnessing a high number of bookings being cancelled, claim industry professionals. They add that fresh enquiries have also stopped coming in.
"The avalanche has developed a sense of insecurity among tourists, as the incident hit a group of trainees from Nehru Institute of Mountaineering (NIM), which is a symbol of excellence in the field," said Bhagwat Semwal, owner of a trekking and mountaineering agency in Uttarkashi.
